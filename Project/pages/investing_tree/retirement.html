Why Retirement Plans are Important

The most effective way to become wealthy is to invest a small amount of money over your full lifetime. By the time you are able to retire, you will be so comfortably. According to Money Magazine, 55% of Americans don't have investments into an IRA or 401(k) account.

What Main Types of Plans are Available?

IRAs

IRAs (individual retirement arrangements) are tax deductible retirement plans which individuals can contribute to with earned income. A traditional IRA is taxed when money is withdrawn, while a Roth IRA is contributed with after-tax dollars, meaning they will be not taxed upon withdrawl.

401(k)s

The 401(k) is a plan that allows employees of corporations to make contributions with their earned income. A traditional 401(k) is taxed when money is withdrawn, while a Roth 401(k) is contributed with after-tax dollars, meaning they will be not taxed upon withdrawl.

Others

403(b)s are retirement plans offered mainly by non-profits and public schools. They are very similar to 401(k)s.

457s are retirement plans for state and local government employees.
